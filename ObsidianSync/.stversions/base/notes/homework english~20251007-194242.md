## ex17

- Fields
    - V → Vocational (профессиональные курсы, FE, колледжи)
    - B → Bachelor’s degree (Undergraduate, обычно 3 года)
    - M → Master’s degree (Postgraduate, около 1 года)
    - P → PhD (Postgraduate, научные исследования)
- Answer:
    - First, students can take a vocational degree, which prepares them for specific professions and gives more practical skills.
    - The next stage is a bachelor’s degree, usually lasting three years. It can be either general or more career-oriented.
    - After that, students may continue with a master’s degree, which takes about one year and provides advanced knowledge in their field.
    - The highest level is the PhD, which is focused on independent research.
At every stage, from vocational training to doctoral study, students can choose either to continue their education or to enter employment.

## 4 Study word-building rules

### a. Nouns

1. The university sends a ready studying invitation to the applicant. (to apply)
2. Our main task is to provide the students with useful information about universities. (to
inform)
3. I had no secretary or assistant. (to assist)
4. We currently provide employment to 17 young professionals. (to employ)
5. For more information, please visit our website. (… + site)

### b. Verbs

1. This is the reason I disagree with you. (do not agree)
2. How do computers communicate on a local network? (communication)
3. We specialize in this area and offer a wide range of products. (specialization)
4. Does that mean that I don't qualify as a programmer? (qualification)
5. I am glad that our library has reopened and I can get books and magazines again. (to
open)
6. My friend told me a very interesting story, but I can’t retell it, I don’t remember
all the facts. (tell)

### c. Adjecrives and adverbs

1. I have high-quality Internet connection. (high / quality)
2. The purpose of vocational training is to equip people to lead active, productive, and
satisfying lives. (vocation)
3. Real life gives you practical skills. (practice)
4. This university attracts students from all over the world who seek to pursue different
courses. (to differ)
5. I worked for a very well-known university. (well/know)

Opposite adjectives: unpopular, impractical, unfriendly, informal, impolite, illogical, responsible, unsatisfied, usefull
